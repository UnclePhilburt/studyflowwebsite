Repeat Infringer Policy

In accordance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. Section 512(i)), StudyFlow Suite has adopted and reasonably implements a policy that provides for the termination of accounts belonging to users who are repeat infringers.

A "repeat infringer" is any user who has been the subject of two or more valid DMCA takedown notices. Upon receipt of a second valid takedown notice for the same account, StudyFlow will:

  • Permanently terminate the user's account
  • Remove all content uploaded by the user from the platform
  • Ban the user from creating new accounts
  • Retain records of the termination for legal compliance
Notice: Users who upload copyrighted material (textbook pages, exam questions, proprietary course materials) risk immediate account suspension on first offense and permanent ban on second offense. StudyFlow takes copyright infringement seriously.

Student Upload Protections

If you upload your own original study notes to StudyFlow, you retain full ownership of your content. By sharing notes with the Nexus, you grant StudyFlow a non-exclusive license to display and distribute your work for educational purposes.

All note downloads include watermarks with your username and a unique transaction ID. This protects your work and provides attribution while discouraging unauthorized redistribution.

Documents created or edited through the Private Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint) and the PDF Editor are stored in your personal cloud storage. You retain full ownership of all documents you create. These documents are private by default and are not shared with other users unless you explicitly choose to upload them to the Nexus, at which point the same content and copyright policies apply.

Download Limits: To prevent abuse and protect contributors, users are limited to 3 note downloads per day. Users must upload at least one verified note per month to maintain "Good Standing" and access community downloads.
