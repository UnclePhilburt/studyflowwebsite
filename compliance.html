1. Missouri GUARD Act (SB 1455) -- Age Verification Compliant

Missouri's GUARD Act takes effect August 28, 2026 and requires age verification and AI disclosure for platforms using artificial intelligence with users under 18. StudyFlow Suite is built to exceed these requirements.

Phase 1: Self-Certification (Operational)
All users must certify they are 18 years or older at registration. The platform enforces an 18+ age gate on account creation. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) sources, university-specific notes, and Nexus library access are gated behind verified .edu email domains, ensuring the academic knowledge base remains a closed-loop environment for adult college students.
Phase 2: Government-Grade ID Verification (Pre-Deadline)
Integration with Veratad, a government-grade identity verification API, is scheduled for completion by June 2026 -- two months ahead of the August 28, 2026 legal deadline. Veratad provides 2.5 Grade Level age verification at approximately $0.30 per student verified annually. This upgrade ensures compliance with the full letter of the GUARD Act's ID-based verification mandate.
AI Disclosure and Human-in-the-Loop
Every AI interaction begins with a mandatory disclosure: "You are chatting with StudyFlow AI, an artificial intelligence assistant. I am not a human." A rolling 30-minute interval disclosure automatically reminds users they are interacting with AI. The AI is constrained to answering from student-uploaded source material via RAG -- it does not generate content from general internet knowledge unless no relevant notes are found, in which case this is explicitly disclosed.
2. AI Transparency and Data Retention (SB 1324) Compliant

Missouri SB 1324 requires AI content provenance marking and long-term record retention. StudyFlow Suite implements this at the database level with immutable audit logging.

7-Year Provenance Archive
Every AI interaction is written to the ai_provenance_logs table in PostgreSQL (Supabase) with the following fields: user_id, conversation_id, prompt_text (full user query), response_text (full AI output), sources_used (JSON array of note IDs and filenames the AI cited), model_used (e.g., gemini-3.1-flash-lite-preview), response_time_ms, and ip_address. These records are retained for a minimum of 7 years. Conversations are soft-deleted (hidden from the UI) but the underlying provenance log is never purged during the retention window.
AI Content Marking
All AI-generated text includes visible provenance headers ("AI-Generated Content"). The StudyFlow Assistant sidebar in ONLYOFFICE automatically labels all AI output. Steganographic watermarking embeds invisible zero-width Unicode characters encoding "STUDYFLOW-AI" into AI responses for machine-readable provenance detection. When users copy AI-generated text to the clipboard, a provenance attribution line is automatically appended.
Source Traceability via RAG
Every AI response is linked to the specific note chunks that informed it via the sources_used field in the provenance log. Each source reference includes the note_id (UUID), filename, and similarity score from the pgvector semantic search. This creates a complete chain of custody: any AI output can be traced back to the exact uploaded document, the exact text chunk, and the user who uploaded it -- at any point within the 7-year retention window.
Upload Audit Trail
Every file upload is logged with SHA-256 hash (tamper detection), timestamp, file size, IP address, user ID, and sharing preference. This audit trail enables verification that a file has not been modified after upload and provides a forensic record for DMCA or academic integrity investigations.
3. Data Sovereignty and Security Active
Self-Hosted Document Server
The Private Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint) is powered by ONLYOFFICE Document Server, self-hosted on a dedicated DigitalOcean droplet under StudyFlow's sole control. Student documents are rendered, edited, and stored entirely on company-controlled infrastructure -- they are never processed by Google Docs, Microsoft 365, or any third-party SaaS document provider. This eliminates the data-sharing and terms-of-service risks inherent in third-party cloud document suites.
Encryption
In transit: All connections enforce TLS 1.2+ via HTTPS. No unencrypted HTTP connections are accepted. At rest: Database storage on Supabase uses AES-256 encryption. File storage (Supabase Storage, S3-compatible) encrypts objects at rest. The ONLYOFFICE server encrypts document storage on the DigitalOcean volume.
Authentication and Access Control
Supabase Auth handles user authentication with bcrypt password hashing and JWT session tokens. PostgreSQL Row-Level Security (RLS) is enforced on every user-facing data table -- users can only query their own data at the database level, regardless of application-layer logic. Study Group data is scoped to group membership via RLS policies. The backend API uses a service role key for server-to-server operations, with all user-facing endpoints validating the JWT bearer token.
Student Data Isolation
Personal notes are private by default. Nexus sharing is opt-in on a per-upload basis via an explicit toggle. Study Group notes are uploaded directly to the group and are only accessible to current group members -- they do not appear in the user's personal notes or the Nexus. When a user leaves a group or is removed, their access to group data is immediately revoked. No student data is sold, shared with advertisers, or used for purposes beyond the service described in the Terms of Service.
Cyber Liability Insurance
Chubb cyber liability policy (A++ rated, AM Best) providing coverage for data breach response costs, forensic investigation, regulatory defense, business interruption, third-party liability claims, ransomware response, and media liability for AI-generated content. This policy protects both the business and any institutional partner's risk exposure.
4. The Nexus -- Content Safety and Copyright Active

The Nexus is StudyFlow's community knowledge network where verified students share notes across universities. Content integrity is maintained through a combination of human review and automated safeguards.

Human-in-the-Loop Content Vetting
Every community upload is manually reviewed by the Founder to verify: content is original student work (not copyrighted textbooks or exam keys), material meets academic integrity standards, no personally identifiable information (PII) is included, and content is appropriately categorized by university and course. This vetting process creates a labeled dataset that will train an AI content classifier for automated review at scale.
DMCA Compliance
Full Digital Millennium Copyright Act compliance with a published DMCA Policy. Takedown requests are processed within 48 hours. Counter-notification support and repeat infringer policies are in place. All uploads are logged with SHA-256 hashes for forensic verification in copyright disputes.
Wikipedia Content Attribution
When AI responses cite Wikipedia articles, the source is explicitly identified with a direct link and a CC BY-SA 4.0 license notice including the retrieval date. Wikipedia sources are tracked separately from student notes in the provenance log and are clearly labeled in the UI.

7. Data Deletion and User Rights
Account Deletion
Users can request deletion of their account and all associated data at any time. Personal notes, documents, and profile data are permanently removed. Conversations and AI interactions are soft-deleted (hidden from the user interface) but retained in the provenance log for the 7-year compliance window required by SB 1324. Notes previously shared with the Nexus may remain as per the license granted at upload.
Study Group Data
When a Study Group is deleted by its owner, all associated data is permanently removed: messages, shared files (deleted from storage), scheduled events, and membership records. When a member leaves or is removed, their access is immediately revoked. Group chat is between users only (no AI), so the 7-year SB 1324 retention requirement does not apply to group messages.
